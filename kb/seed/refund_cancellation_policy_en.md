# Refund & Cancellation Policy

This document explains Acme Cloud Suite's refund guarantee, how proration works when you change
plans, and what happens to your data if you cancel.

## 14-day money-back guarantee

If you are not satisfied with Acme Cloud Suite, you can request a full refund within **14 days
of your first purchase** on a paid plan (Starter or Pro, monthly or annual billing). To request
a refund, contact support from **Settings > Billing > Get Help** or email
support@acmecloudsuite.example.com within the 14-day window. Refunds under this guarantee apply
only to a customer's **first** paid purchase — it is not renewed with every subsequent billing
cycle or plan change.

## Upgrades are prorated immediately

When you upgrade your plan (for example, Free to Starter, Starter to Pro, or monthly to annual),
the change takes effect immediately. The cost difference for the remainder of your current
billing cycle is calculated on a prorated basis and charged right away, so you get instant
access to the higher plan's user limits, storage, and support SLA.

## Downgrades are not refunded mid-cycle

When you downgrade your plan (for example, Pro to Starter), the change is scheduled to take
effect at the start of your **next** billing cycle. You keep your current (higher) plan's
features until then, but no partial refund is issued for downgrading before the cycle ends —
you've already paid for that cycle at the original plan level.

## Canceling your account

You can cancel your subscription at any time from **Settings > Billing > Cancel Subscription**.
Cancellation stops future billing. If you cancel partway through a paid billing cycle, the same
proration rules above apply: cancellation is treated like a downgrade to no plan, so it is not
refunded for the remaining time in the current cycle, unless you are still within the 14-day
money-back window on your first purchase.

## What happens to your data after cancellation

After you cancel, Acme Inc. retains your account data for **90 days**. During this period, you
can reactivate your account and pick up exactly where you left off — your projects, tasks,
files, and settings are restored. After the 90-day retention period ends, your account data is
**permanently deleted** and cannot be recovered.

If you plan to cancel and might want your data later, export what you need before canceling, or
reactivate within the 90-day window.

## Enterprise cancellations

Enterprise plan cancellations, refunds, and data retention terms may be adjusted in a custom
contract negotiated with your account manager; where no custom terms are specified, the standard
policy above (including the 90-day data retention period) applies.
